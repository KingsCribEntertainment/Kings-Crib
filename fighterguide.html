How to fight like a pro

select

Choose a character and stick with them. This allows you to develop a deep understanding of their moves, combos, and strengths and weaknesses.

Learn the fundamentals: Before diving into advanced techniques, it's important to learn the basics of the game such as blocking, movement, and attacking. Spend time in practice mode learning how to move around the stage, block incoming attacks, and deliver basic combos.

fundamentals

mastery

Master your character's moves: Once you've learned the basics, focus on mastering your character's moves. Spend time in practice mode executing their special moves, combos, and unique attacks. Try to understand the purpose of each move and how it can be used in different situations.

Understand frame data: Frame data is the amount of time it takes for a move to start up, how long it lasts, and how much recovery time there is after it. Understanding frame data is essential to optimizing your combos and knowing when to punish your opponent's moves.

frame data

practice

Practice, practice, practice: Practice is key to becoming good at any fighting game. Spend time in practice mode perfecting your combos, movement, and defense. It's also a good idea to play against other players online or in local tournaments to gain experience and test your skills.

Watch and learn: Watch high-level players of your character and take note of their playstyle, combos, and strategies. You can also watch replays of your own matches to identify areas of weakness and areas to improve on.

observe

discipline

Stay patient and stay focused: Fighting games require a lot of practice and can be frustrating at times. Stay patient and don't give up. Focus on small improvements and celebrate each victory, no matter how small.

Overall, becoming good at fighting games takes time, practice, and dedication. By following these tips and focusing on your fundamentals, you can become a skilled player and enjoy the competitive nature of the game.
